# Product Overview

Lingofy is a B2C Educational Technology (EdTech) application designed to disrupt the traditional language learning market by embedding learning mechanics directly into music streaming.

## 1. User Personas

### "The Passive Listener" (Emma, 22)
- **Goal:** Wants to learn Spanish but lacks the discipline for daily Duolingo streaks.
- **Behavior:** Listens to Bad Bunny on Spotify every commute.
- **Value Prop:** Emma opens Lingofy during her commute. She sees real-time translations of the lyrics. She clicks a word she doesn't know, saves it to her vocabulary, and closes the app. Effortless learning.

### "The Pronunciation Perfectionist" (Kenji, 28)
- **Goal:** Wants to reduce his accent in English to get a promotion at work.
- **Behavior:** Sings along to Coldplay songs.
- **Value Prop:** Kenji uses **Shadowing Mode**. He records himself singing a line, and the Pronunciation Coach tells him his 'R' sounds were 40% inaccurate, giving him actionable advice to fix his tongue placement.

## 2. Core Use Cases

- **Casual Discovery:** Listening to a new foreign song and instantly understanding its poetic meaning without switching to a browser.
- **Active Shadowing:** Repeating verses rapidly to build mouth muscle memory.
- **Vocabulary Building:** Utilizing the Spaced Repetition System (SRS) dashboard to review words encountered in songs.

## 4. Success Metrics (KPIs)

- **Daily Active Users (DAU) / Monthly Active Users (MAU):** Sticky factor.
- **Average Session Length:** Target is >15 minutes (approx. 4-5 songs).
- **Words Mastered per User:** Indicates actual educational value provided.
- **Free-to-Paid Conversion Rate:** Target > 4%.

## 5. User Experience (UX) Philosophy

1. **Music First:** The app must NEVER interrupt the music playback. Learning overlays are strictly opt-in.
2. **Zero Loading Screens:** Heavy caching and optimistic UI updates ensure the user never stares at a spinner.
3. **Immersive Aesthetics:** The UI must feel like a premium music player (glassmorphism, dynamic colors based on album art) rather than a boring textbook app.
